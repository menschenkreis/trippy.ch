Fractal Dreams

Gently evolving Julia set fractals bathed in DMT-inspired colors. Pinch to zoom infinitely, drag two fingers to pan. One finger warps the shape.

Interpretation

Fractals are the geometry of nature — found in ferns, river deltas, lightning bolts, and, some would say, the fabric of consciousness itself. The Julia set is a particular family of fractals where a simple mathematical rule, repeated endlessly, produces structures of staggering complexity. This piece lets that rule drift slowly over time, so the fractal morphs through an endless sequence of forms — each one a frozen moment from an infinite landscape. The DMT-inspired palette of deep indigos, electric magentas, and shimmering golds mirrors the visual language often reported in breakthrough psychedelic experiences: geometric, self-similar, and alive with color.

How it's done

A "Julia set" works by taking a point on screen and applying a simple rule over and over: square it, add a fixed number, repeat. Depending on the starting point, this either spirals away to infinity or stays bounded — and the boundary between those two behaviors is the fractal. The "fixed number" (called the Julia constant) determines the shape. In this experiment, that constant slowly drifts in a circle, which is why the fractal morphs continuously. Each pixel is colored based on how many iterations it took to escape — and those iteration counts are mapped to colors from a DMT-inspired palette. Pinch to zoom in and you'll discover ever-finer detail — fractals are literally infinite, so there's always more to see. Drag with two fingers to pan around. One finger still warps the Julia constant, changing the shape itself. All of this runs on your GPU as a fragment shader at 60 fps.
