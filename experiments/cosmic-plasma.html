Cosmic Plasma

A slowly morphing fractal plasma field that breathes with layered noise. Move your finger or cursor to warp the field.

Interpretation

Plasma fields are one of the classic visuals of the psychedelic experience — organic, flowing, and alive. This piece mimics that feeling: layers of noise pile up like thoughts stacking on top of each other, each one slightly warped by the last. The colors drift between deep purples, electric teals, and soft pinks, much like the shifting hues behind closed eyes. It responds to your touch because the best trips are the ones you participate in.

How it's done

The visual is generated entirely by your device's graphics card (GPU) using a technique called a "fragment shader" — a tiny program that runs for every single pixel on screen, calculating its color in real time. At its core, we layer multiple octaves of "noise" (a way to generate smooth, natural-looking randomness) on top of each other — think of it like zooming into clouds: the big shapes contain smaller shapes, which contain even smaller ones. Your cursor position subtly shifts the pattern, creating the interactive warp effect. All of this runs at 60 frames per second, calculated fresh every frame.
